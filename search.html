Search Guide

Multi-word, Exact Phrase Searching

Wrapping quote characters can be included to narrow results when searching for locations, names of persons, or other exact phrases.

Examples
  • "San Francisco" will yield results where the exact phrase, San Francisco, exists, excluding results that may also include the word San.
  • "Missouri Compromise" will yield more specific results than Missouri Compromise, excluding results that may also include the use of Missouri and Compromise independent of each other.
  • "James Buchanan" will yield more specific results than James Buchanan, excluding results that may also include other persons with a first name James.

Wildcard Searching

Using asterisks can expand results to include words that include a portion of the substring being searched.

Examples
  • Suffix searching: searching annex* would include results where the word annexation and annexed are also included.
  • Prefix searching: searching *charge would include results where the word discharge is also included.
  • Words that contain substrings: Searching for *slave* would also include results where the word slaves, enslaved and slavery are mentioned.
  • Single character wildcard searches: Searching for te?t will include results where the term test or text appears.
  • Multi character wildcard searches: Searching for ch*ss will include results where the term Childress or cheapness appear.

Boolean Operators

The following boolean operators can be used to refine your search: AND, OR, NOT, +, and -.

Examples
  • Eliza Caldwell AND mule will yield results where both the term Caldwell and the term mule are present in the document.
  • Caldwell OR mule will yield results where the term Caldwell or the term mule are present in the document.
  • Caldwell NOT mule will yield results where the term Caldwell is present in the document but not the term mule.
  • The (+), or required operator, requires that the term after the “+” symbol exist somewhere in the document. (i.e. +Caldwell mule)
  • The (-), or prohibit operator, excludes documents that include the term or phrase that appears after the “-” symbol. (i.e. Caldwell -mule)

Groupings

Parentheses around groupings can be used to control the boolean logic of a query.

Examples
  • (Mississippi OR “New Orleans”) AND Walker will return results where the term Mississippi or the phrase New Orleans is present and the term Walker is required.
  • (Mississippi AND “New Orleans”) OR Walker will return results where the term Mississippi and the phrase New Orleans are present but the term Walker is optional.
  • (Mississippi OR “New Orleans”) NOT Walker will return results where the term Mississippi or the phrase New Orleans is present but the term Walker is prohibited.

Escape Characters

There are several characters that have special meaning that must be escaped in order to include them in searches. These include: + - && || ! ( ) { } [ ] ^ " ~ * ? : \. To include these in a search, use a \ before the character.

Examples
  • "Imm\[e\]diately" will return results where the exact term "Imm\[e\]diately" is present.
  • Imm\[e\]diately will return results where any parts of the term Imm\[e\]diately is present.
  • Imm[e]diately will return an error or no results.
